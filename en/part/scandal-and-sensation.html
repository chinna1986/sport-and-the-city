Scandal and Sensation

The leader of the Ukrainian team, Olha Korobka, was disqualified until 2015 for use of banned substances, and she was removed of her bronze medal from the 2011 world championships. However, the matter is not completely clear, because her colleagues are convinced that Olha did not consciously take drugs or doping agents.

Lance Armstrong, a symbol of strength of will and endurance in world athletics. After surviving cancer he became a 7-time champion of the Tour de France (from 1999-2005). He also supported social projects in the fight against cancer. In 2010 a judicial trial in suspicion of doping was initiated against Lance Armstrong. The case lasted until 2012, when the athlete was acquitted of all accusations.

«The Moggi Affair» – the most sensational corruption scandal in world football in 2006 in Italy. So called after the head manager of the Turin football club «Juventus,» Luciano Moggi, who was connected with match-fixing with football referees for profit. The Italian clubs «Lazzio,» «Milan,» and «Fiorentina» also figured in the affair. The champion of the Italian clubs—Juventus—was removed of its title and sent to B-level, where it started the new season with minus-30 points. Luciano Moggi received a lifetime disqualification from Italian football.
